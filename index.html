About Me

I am a Front-End Web Developer at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) in Golden, CO. I am the primary coder for 26 NREL.gov subsites, 36 intranet subsites, and two U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy websites. Prior to NREL, I was a Web Editor for Leidos - the prime contractor for the National Science Foundation's (NSF) United States Antarctic Program (USAP). My most notable task while in the program involved traveling to South Pole Station to roll out the new intranet for station personnel.

In my daily work-related tasking, I use HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap 3.3 and 4, XML, and JavaScript. I am proficient in Reactstrap, Node.js, and MySQL. I have experience using Git, OU Campus CMS - OmniUpdate, and an in-house Coldfusion CMS for website development. My prefered code editor is Visual Studio Code; however, I am comfortable using Adobe Dreamweaver and Notepad++. I am skilled in Adobe Photoshop, and it is my preferred graphic and image editing software. In my previous print design experience, I used Adobe InDesign to create brochures, business cards, and my resume amongst other items.

I recently graduated from University of Denver's Coding Boot Camp in March 2020. I have a Bachelor of Science degree in English - Professional Writing with a minor in Human Computer Interaction/User Experience (HCI/UX) from the University of North Alabama (UNA). Prior to recieving my bachelor's degree, I graduated from Calhoun Community College with an Associate of Applied Science degree in Business Administration - Paralegal Studies.
