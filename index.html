About Me

I am a Frontend Web Developer at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) in Golden, Colorado, where I serve as the primary coder for various NREL.gov subsites, intranet sites, and U.S. Department of Energy websites. Previously, I was a Web Editor for Leidos, supporting the National Science Foundation's U.S. Antarctic Program (USAP), including a notable deployment to the South Pole to launch a new intranet for station personnel.

My expertise includes HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, XML, Java, and JavaScript, along with proficiency in Reactstrap, Node.js, MySQL, and Git. I am skilled in Visual Studio Code, Adobe Creative Cloud, and content management systems (CMS) like Sitefinity, OU Campus, and an in-house ColdFusion-based platform.

My interdisciplinary foundation strengthens both the technical and creative aspects of my work. I hold a Full Stack Development certificate from the University of Denver, a B.S. in English – Professional Writing with a minor in Human-Computer Interaction/User Experience (HCI/UX), and an A.S. in Business Administration – Paralegal Studies. This diverse academic background informs my approach to front-end development, enabling me to craft user-centered digital experiences with clarity, precision, and strategic insight.

Currently, I am pursuing an M.A. in Art and Technology at the University of Oklahoma (expected August 2025) to deepen my knowledge of object-oriented programming while exploring computational media, data visualization, and interactive design.
